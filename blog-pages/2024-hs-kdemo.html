2024 Highschool Karate Demonstration

8th August, 2024

These past two weeks, I've felt more human than I ever did last year. I experienced a variety of emotions, ranging from excitement to nervousness.

The Talent show was much more than "showing off my skills". It had nothing to do with that. It was an opportunity to find myself again. After injurying myself I lost all my confidence and sense of identity. I thought I couldn't return to the passionate person I once was. For the longest time, I felt emotionally number, and I lost my care for everyone and everything. I didn't feel human or alive. It wasn't until someone made me realise how much I was going through that I finally broke down and witnessed an overwhelming wave of sadness. I realised I had given up fighting for myself, accepting that I'd be weak forever. But when I finally had surgery, I promised that I'd start trying again and do my best no matter what.

This time last year, I would have been helplessly stuck in my room, recovering from surgery with my mum looking after me. While I was excited to see where this new journey would take me, I was also incredibly scared. I would constantly imagine myself falling down and re-injuring myself, and also being frustrated and disappointed in myself for not being able to do what I could do before my injury. I'm still haunted by these thoughts. I am my greatest critic and I can be incredibly harsh on myself. I decided to join the talent show to challenge these thoughts and prove to myself that with time and patience, I CAN be better than who I was before my injury.

Breaking two concrete bricks actually has a story itself too. In a first-degree black belt grading, you're required to break one brick, and I failed twice before finally breaking it. I had so much anger in me and a lot of things upset me back then, so I tried to exert those emotions onto the concrete. I believe that's why I failed. I was trying to break the concrete with my emotions, and I had no control over them. I wish I reflected on my journey rather than letting hate take over.
Initially, I only planned to break one slab for the demonstration. I attempted breaking one beforehand, and then thought "Why not break two?" I wanted to push myself further and show that I had truly overcome my fears and doubts. I was SUPER unsure and nervous, but I still wanted to try. I told myself over and over again, that I could totally embarrass myself in front of the school or go home feeling like a champion. Today, with my genuine heart and soul, I successfully broke two.

There were lots of things I wanted to include and perfect in this performance. The process of putting this together allowed me to accept that I'm not at my best YET and still need more time!

I had a lot of fun preparing this, and I'm grateful to have had my friends help out too! Especially in our last year of highschool. Even though my performance wasn't perfect, I'm still happy knowing how much time and effort I put into it. I guess I can say I've found myself again. I have always been a martial artist (even though I'm a bit shy to show it), and my journey as one never ended when I tore my ACL. I'm looking forward to seeing what I achieve next.
